1. Purpose

Hefestus operates the Program to receive introductions to prospective customers for its automation machinery from independent referrers. In connection with the Referrer's participation, Hefestus may disclose Confidential Information to the Referrer, and the Referrer may use such Confidential Information solely to identify, evaluate, and introduce prospective leads under the Program (the "Permitted Use"). This Agreement governs that disclosure. It does not itself set, and should not be read as setting, the referral fee amounts, payment method, or other commercial terms of the Program; those are governed separately by the Referral Partner Agreement signed in Step 4.

2. Confidential Information

"Confidential Information" means any and all technical and non-technical information and/or data, including but not limited to business plans, product samples and specifications, technical and non-technical materials, formulas, prototypes, ideas, methods, techniques, processes and projections, memoranda, analyses, notes, legal documents, improvements, patents (whether pending or duly registered), designs, finances, research, development, personnel, or any derivative, modification or elaboration of any such Confidential Information in whatever form, related to the past, current, future or proposed operations, products, services and business of Hefestus, its business partners, suppliers, licensors and customers. Confidential Information specifically includes, without limitation: (a) the existence, structure, and rates of the Program's referral fees and commission terms; (b) any information Hefestus discloses to the Referrer to confirm whether a prospective introduction qualifies as a lead under the Program, including whether a given company or contact already has an existing relationship or contact history with Hefestus sales or marketing; and (c) any non-public information about Hefestus's products, pricing, or roadmap shared with the Referrer in the course of the Program.

Confidential Information excludes information that the Recipient can prove by credible evidence: (i) was already known to Recipient prior to disclosure; (ii) is independently developed by or for Recipient without reference to or use of the Confidential Information; or (iii) at the time of disclosure is generally available to the public or thereafter becomes generally available to the public other than through a breach of this Agreement caused by an act or omission of the Recipient.

3. Limitations on Use and Disclosure

Recipient shall hold and maintain the Confidential Information in strict confidence and protect it against disclosure, using it for no purpose other than the Permitted Use. Recipient shall only permit access to Confidential Information to those of its Representatives having a need to know it in connection with the Permitted Use, who are aware of this Agreement and bound by confidentiality obligations no less restrictive than these terms. Recipient is liable for any breach by its Representatives.

4. Disclosure Required by Law

Recipient may disclose Confidential Information pursuant to a court order or as otherwise required by law, provided Recipient gives Hefestus reasonable advance notice where possible, cooperates with any effort to limit the scope of disclosure, and discloses only the portion legally required. All other Confidential Information remains protected.

5. Standard of Care

Recipient shall use at least a reasonable degree of care to prevent unauthorized use, dissemination, or copying of the Confidential Information, no less than the care it uses for its own confidential information, and shall notify Hefestus immediately of any loss or unauthorized disclosure or use.

6. Return of Confidential Information

Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement, or upon Hefestus's written request, Recipient shall promptly return or destroy all documents and materials representing the Confidential Information and all copies thereof.

Confidential Information shall not be reproduced except as required for the Permitted Use, and any reproduction remains Hefestus's property. Recipient shall not, and shall not permit any third party to, modify, reverse engineer, decompile, or create derivative works from any Confidential Information.

9. Term and Termination

This Agreement is effective from the Effective Date and expires one (1) year thereafter, or upon the end of Recipient's active participation in the Program if later, or may be terminated by either party upon thirty (30) days' written notice. Recipient's confidentiality obligations survive expiration or termination for five (5) years and bind Recipient's heirs, successors, and assigns.

10. Relationship of the Parties; Non-Circumvention

The Referrer participates in the Program solely as an independent introducer, not as an employee, agent, partner, or legal representative of Hefestus, and has no authority to make commitments or representations on Hefestus's behalf. 13. Assignment

Referral process

Your introduction qualifies if the contact hasn't spoken to Hefestus sales or marketing in the last 6 months. We check this against our CRM and log it under your name the moment you send it over. To stay qualified, the deal has to close within 12 months of that date, a referral doesn't stay open indefinitely.

Reward

AuraX pays $5,000 flat, LumineX pays $1,500 flat, other Hefestus machinery pays 3% of sale price, and AutoTube pays $3,500 flat, per closed sale. Paid in full once your referred deal closes, via wire transfer or credit card, we'll collect payout details securely after that, never as part of sign-up.

No employment, no exclusivity

This doesn't make you an employee or agent of Hefestus. Step away any time with written notice; anything already tracked in HubSpot before you do stays payable on qualifying close.

Governing law

Governed by the laws of the State of Nevada. Any dispute is handled in the courts of Clark County, Nevada.
